Objections

The questions everyone asks
before they switch.

01

"I'm already in a contract with another provider."

Check your exit date. If your provider is raising prices — most do annually — you likely have a right to exit without penalty during the notice period. The £4/month annual broadband increase most providers apply each March is typically a valid exit trigger. Enter your renewal date and we'll confirm where you stand.

02

"My flatmates are on different networks and won't switch."

They don't need to switch. Adding a SIM to your EE One account is not switching networks — it's a SIM-only plan add-on. They keep their existing number. No new phone contract. No handover. Takes four minutes online. If they move out, cancels without penalty.

03

"What if I have to move out before 24 months?"

The move-with-us guarantee means your contract follows you to your next address on the same terms. If EE Full Fibre isn't available at your new address, you'll pay a standard exit fee — but the same applies to every provider. We'll confirm coverage before you commit.

04

"Are they going to put the price up halfway through?"

EE applies an annual increase of £4/month on broadband each March. This is disclosed upfront — you'll see it on the pricing page and in this document. It is not a surprise. If any provider claims prices won't change: read their small print more carefully.
