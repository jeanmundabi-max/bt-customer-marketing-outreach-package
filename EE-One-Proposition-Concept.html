EE One / Proposition Gap Diagnosis
April 2026
Pre-Interview Strategic Analysis

The first-renter segment is EE One's largest unaddressed growth lever

EE One is a strong product in the wrong conversation. This analysis identifies three proposition gaps, four funnel leaks, and a 90-day plan to own the move-in moment before any competitor does.

Role Customer Marketing Manager candidate

01

Who EE One is for right now

EE One is positioned as "the perfect plan for your household." The page assumes the visitor already knows what EE One is, why bundling matters, and that they want EE specifically. That is a Solution Aware customer — settled, deliberate, already evaluating EE.

The hero CTA is "Create your EE One today." The social proof is award claims. The copy says "exclusive household savings" and "unlimited data boost on eligible existing mobile plans." Every word is written for someone who has already decided.

There is no before-and-after. No savings calculator. No scenario framing for someone who is working out their telecoms setup for the first time. No move-in language. The page works for one segment of the market and completely misses the other.

These are the people making broadband decisions right now. And they are not Solution Aware. They are Problem Aware — they know paying two bills for broadband and mobile is annoying, but they do not know EE One exists or why it solves their specific situation.

More importantly: they are making a 24-month decision with no incumbent provider, no loyalty, and no sunk cost bias. They are the easiest possible acquisition target — if the proposition meets them where they are.

"Moved home on 12th July. Two weeks later my broadband is still not working. I've spent over 20 hours speaking to technical support. So much for 'Just plug in at your new address and you're off.'"

The experience of setting up EE at a new home is EE's most visible failure point. It is also the exact moment a first-renter is forming their 24-month opinion of a brand.

03

What the current page does wrong

F1

The hero doesn't speak to anyone specific

"The perfect plan for your household" could be Sky, Vodafone, or Virgin Media. A 22-year-old who just signed their first tenancy agreement lands on that page and sees nothing that confirms this is for them. They bounce. Not because EE One is wrong for them — but because the page doesn't know them.

F2

The moving-home penalty is invisible on the acquisition page

The EE Community forum has multiple threads where customers describe being put on new 24-month contracts at higher prices when they move home (one case: £44.99 to £59.99, with a £360 cancellation fee). This is EE's most visible negative word-of-mouth. The page that is supposed to acquire new customers says nothing about it. Every first-renter who has heard one of these stories has an objection that is never answered.

F3

The saving is abstract without a calculator

"Exclusive household savings of up to £20 a month" is a claim with no anchor. A first-renter paying ~£31 for broadband and ~£39 for mobile separately (UK average, 18-24 cohort) is spending ~£70/month. EE One at £41.99 saves them ~£27/month — £327 per year. The page never shows this. The saving is invisible.

04

The three proposition gaps

GAP 1

No move-in moment targeting

There is no EE One campaign, content strategy, or partnership designed to intercept the first-renter at the moment of maximum receptivity — the tenancy agreement signing, the SpareRoom confirmation, the Rightmove "move soon" date. EE's JD explicitly calls out "youth segment strategies" as a priority. But right now, the customer making a 24-month broadband decision in their first flat is choosing from a comparison table where EE looks more expensive than Vodafone and Virgin.

GAP 2

No first-renter packaging

EE One's household SIM discount (up to £20/month off for additional SIMs) is the right answer to the shared-flat problem. It already exists in the product. But it is not packaged for a 22-year-old who wants to say to three flatmates: "Get on my EE One account and your SIM is from £12/month." No flatmate framing. No household total calculator. No "add your flat to EE One" call to action.

GAP 3

No moving-home guarantee

First-renters move frequently. Moving home with EE currently triggers a new 24-month contract at a higher price. This is the single biggest churn accelerant in the 18-30 segment and generates the most visible negative word-of-mouth. A "move with us for free" guarantee — or a promise that your contract terms stay when you move — would be a genuinely differentiated claim. No competitor offers it. The fact that EE's current policy is the opposite makes it both a commercial problem and a first-mover positioning opportunity.

06

The proposition concept — EE One First Move

EE One First Move

An extension of the existing EE One product — positioned specifically for 18-30 first-renters at the point of moving in. No new infrastructure. No new pricing tier. A proposition, not a product.

Move-in priority installation: Broadband live within 5 days of your key collection date — not 5 days from order. Anchored to the move, not the system.
Flatmate SIMs from £12/month: Already exists in EE One. Packaged and named — with a household calculator that shows the combined saving.
Move-with-us guarantee: When you move home within the 24-month term, your contract terms stay the same. No new contract. No price reset. This reverses EE's single biggest churn trigger in the 18-30 segment.
Student crossover pipeline: Students with Student Beans verification who are moving into their first post-university flat: first 3 months at student-discounted rate, then step up to EE One standard.

This requires no new product development. It requires a segment landing page, a content strategy at the move-in moment, a flatmate-framing of the existing household SIM discount, and a policy clarification on moving home.

07

First 90 days

Wks 1–4

Understand and measure

Audit the 18-30 customer journey from search to sign-up. Pull NPS data for this cohort specifically. Quantify moving-home churn: how many 18-30 customers lapse at home-move vs renew? What is the revenue at stake? Meet Rachel Ward's product team on the move-with-us guarantee feasibility.

Wks 5–8

Build the segment case

Write the business case for EE One First Move: TAM, LTV, ARPU uplift vs single-product customers, CAC via move-in partnerships vs comparison sites. Prototype the segment landing page. Scope the SpareRoom / Rightmove partnership. Test 3 hero headline variants on the existing EE One page — measure bounce rate and conversion.

Wks 9–12

Launch the first version

Publish the segment landing page. Launch content SEO targeting "broadband moving flat UK" and related queries. Pilot the student-to-EE-One pipeline via email to 18-30 existing EE mobile customers on student plans. Brief PR on the move-with-us guarantee — it is a genuinely newsworthy claim in UK broadband.

The acquisition advantage: first-renters have no incumbent provider. Zero win-back cost. A move-in moment partnership with SpareRoom (20M+ users) or Rightmove reaches them at higher intent than any comparison site, at a fraction of the CAC.

The retention advantage: reversing the moving-home penalty converts EE's most visible churn trigger into a loyalty signal. The forum complaints currently generating negative word-of-mouth are a fixable commercial policy — not a product issue.
