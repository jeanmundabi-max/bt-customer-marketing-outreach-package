Leak Map — Four Revenue Drains
LEAK 01 High volume

The Wrong Visitor

EE One is not in the search conversation of people who are about to move. It appears for brand searches only. First-renters searching "broadband moving flat" find comparison sites — where EE One loses on headline price (£41.99 vs Vodafone £24, Virgin £26).

Invest in content and move-in moment partnerships (SpareRoom, Rightmove). Own the search query before the comparison table.
LEAK 02 High volume

The Generic Hero

"The perfect plan for your household" tells a Problem Aware visitor nothing. A 22-year-old moving into their first flat doesn't see themselves. They bounce because the page appears to be for a settled family — not for them.

Segment the hero. A first-renter landing page says: "Your first flat. One bill. Less than you're paying now."
LEAK 03 Med-high

The Moving Home Silence

EE's biggest community complaint — moving home triggers a new 24-month contract at higher price (one customer: £44.99 to £59.99, plus £360 cancellation fee) — is completely invisible on the purchase page. Every prospect who has read a forum post has an unanswered objection.

Add FAQ or callout addressing the moving-home policy. "Move with us for free" as a guarantee would be genuinely differentiated in the market.
LEAK 04 Medium

No Savings Calculator

The page claims "exclusive household savings" but never shows what those savings are. "Up to £480 over two years" is abstract. A first-renter paying ~£30 broadband + ~£39 mobile = ~£69/month doesn't know EE One at £41.99 saves them ~£27/month.

Interactive calculator: enter current broadband cost + mobile cost. Show monthly saving. Show annual equivalent (£324/year is not abstract).
